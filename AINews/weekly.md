**India Embracing Artificial Intelligence for Future Growth**

* India is leveraging AI to transform various sectors, including tourism, education, and logistics, positioning itself as a global leader in sustainable and data-driven tourism.
* The country's AI-driven tourism solutions, such as smart travel planning and personalized guest experiences, are expected to elevate India as a global tourism leader.
* The government's efforts to adopt AI are part of its broader vision for "Viksit Bharat 2047," aiming to make India a developed nation by 2047.

**Indian IT Stocks Decline Amid AI Disruption Fears**

* Top Indian IT stocks saw significant declines following the release of new AI-based tools by Anthropic, which promise to automate tasks previously handled by software services firms.
* The AI tools, including data migration and legal analysis, could potentially replace basic development and testing tasks currently performed by entry-level IT staff in India.
* Analysts warn that Indian IT firms could be particularly hard hit due to the potential for AI-driven disruption.

**AI Developments in India: Opportunities and Challenges**

* Anthropic has expanded its operations in India, appointing a former Microsoft India managing director to lead its operations in the country.
* However, a local software company has filed a court complaint over Anthropic's use of the name, highlighting the potential for conflicts between local incumbents and global AI firms.
* India's growing importance to global AI companies underscores the need for the country to develop its own AI capabilities and address the challenges posed by emerging technologies.

**Telstra-Accenture Data and AI Joint Venture to Cut Positions**

**Meta Fined in India Over WhatsApp Data-Sharing Practices**

* India's Supreme Court has warned Meta that it could once again bar WhatsApp from sharing user data with other Meta platforms for advertising purposes.
* The case carries significant weight for Meta, given India is its largest market globally.
* The dispute over WhatsApp's data-sharing practices has a long history, with Meta being fined US$25.4 million in 2024.
